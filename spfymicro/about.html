About Us

We are group of four Electrical and Computer undergraduates working towards our degrees that we will be completing in the Spring of 2017. With that, graduating seniors at UCSC are tasked with completing a year long design project that has the opportunity to teach teamwork and engineering application. The project these students have selected is sponsored by Professor Ali A. Yanik and his Optical research team as they act as both mentors and the client.

Our Goal

Fluorescence microscopy has become an essential tool in biomedical science for the detection, diagnosis, and treatment of various diseases. Fluorescence microscopy allows for the detection and imaging of nano-sized objects far beyond the capabilities of direct light, or brightfield, microscopy. However, optical imaging of nano-sized objects using traditional methods is challenging; commercial fluorescence microscopy systems used for nanoscale optical imaging, are cost prohibitive, require training, and is limited to lab based settings due to its large size.
This project aims to address the financial, technical, and distributive barriers that prevents individuals from being able to use a fluorescence microscopy system by designing a low cost, user friendly, and compact portable fluorescence microscopy platform that attaches to a smartphone for imaging nano sized bioparticles, such as viruses, along with a user interface and processing software to count and categorize the imaged bioparticles.
This involves the research, design, and implementation of an opto-mechanical microscopy platform which attaches to a smartphone; consisting of various optical filters, lenses, excitation sources, and translation stages; along with a user interface and processing software to allow for ease of use for the user.
